# Historian and Sensitivity Review Preparation

## Boundary and desired output

This is a sourcing and coordination plan for issue #23. It records no outreach,
availability, engagement, paid work, approval, or sign-off. No person or
organization listed below has been contacted. Fees are **quote required** unless
an adjacent public price is explicitly noted; an adjacent price is not a review
quote.

The work needs two independent functions:

1. A Revolutionary-era historian reviews all current chapter scripts and the
   sources appendix for factual accuracy, evidentiary weight, numbers, and
   material/visual accuracy.
2. Separate sensitivity reviewers assess the Yorktown surrender and Treaty
   epilogue, including Native sovereignty and nation-specific framing, slavery
   and Black Loyalist outcomes, and Loyalist displacement. Historical expertise
   does not substitute for community perspective, and one community reviewer
   does not speak for every affected people.

Written deliverables should identify reviewed version/commit, cue IDs, evidence,
severity, recommended wording, unresolved disagreements, and whether each item
is required before VO. “No comment” is not sign-off.

## Review packet checklist

Prepare once, freeze it to a commit, and give every reviewer the same applicable
materials:

- [ ] `docs/narration-scripts.md`, including stage directions and diegetic lines.
- [ ] `docs/sources.md`, with the review/correction findings left visible.
- [ ] `docs/gdd.md`, `docs/decisions.md`, and `docs/architecture.md` for audience,
      register, historical-integrity, and implementation constraints.
- [ ] A cue-indexed response template with `cue`, `severity`, `finding`,
      `evidence`, `recommended action`, and `VO-blocking?` fields.
- [ ] Still frames or annotated boards for historically specific visuals when
      available; label concepts as concepts, not browser/device evidence.
- [ ] A statement that diegetic lines are dramatized and must not be mistaken for
      primary quotations.
- [ ] A written scope, fee, schedule, confidentiality/publication terms, conflict
      disclosure, and the exact meaning of “sign-off.”
- [ ] Version identifier: branch, commit SHA, date, and checksum or immutable link.

## Lead historian checklist

- [ ] Every chapter and every cue received an explicit disposition, including
      “no factual claim” where applicable.
- [ ] Every number has a defined population, time, unit, and acceptable rounding.
- [ ] Primary evidence is distinguished from later memoir, tradition, museum
      interpretation, and production inference.
- [ ] Disputed matters remain disputed: especially the Lexington first shot,
      Yorktown tune, Rall warning story, and West painting account.
- [ ] Famous quotations are traced to contemporaneous text or labeled as later
      recollection/tradition.
- [ ] Composite claims about unnamed people are replaced by named evidence or
      explicitly qualified.
- [ ] Military ranks, unit types, chain of command, strength, casualties,
      geography, route distance, weather, weapons, and formations are checked.
- [ ] Material and visual notes cover clothing, tools, vessels, artillery,
      documents, buildings, lighting, and anachronistic symbols.
- [ ] Factual corrections are separated from optional interpretive suggestions.
- [ ] Final memo names the reviewed commit and lists unresolved items; written
      sign-off is not inferred from silence or partial review.

## Sensitivity and descendant-community checklist

- [ ] Reviewers may define or narrow the community and expertise they represent;
      no reviewer is described as speaking for an entire people.
- [ ] The Treaty language distinguishes Native nations and sovereignty rather
      than using a single generic “Native” outcome.
- [ ] Nation-specific consequences and alliances are named where the evidence
      permits; the narration avoids disappearance and inevitability framing.
- [ ] Enslaved people, free Black people, Black Loyalists, people evacuated, and
      people returned to enslavers are not collapsed into one category.
- [ ] Loyalists who remained, white refugees, Black Loyalists, and Native allies
      are distinguished; property loss is not equated with enslavement or loss
      of sovereignty.
- [ ] Yorktown surrender choreography, humiliation, violence, and participant
      embodiment are reviewed for what the camera asks the viewer to do and feel.
- [ ] The three Treaty dwell cues are factual, specific, non-editorial, and do
      not imply that the settlement's exclusions ended in 1783.
- [ ] Classroom language is age-appropriate without euphemizing slavery,
      dispossession, death, or coercion.
- [ ] Reviewers can mark harmful framing even when each sentence is narrowly
      factual; disagreements and project decisions remain on file.

## Findings and change-control checklist

- [ ] Triage each finding as `blocker`, `required before VO`, `recommended`, or
      `note`, with a responsible owner.
- [ ] Record `accepted`, `declined with rationale`, or `unresolved`; never record
      approval that was not written by the reviewer.
- [ ] Correct the source appendix and script in separate, cue-scoped commits.
- [ ] Re-run source-link and cue/chapter coverage checks after every factual edit.
- [ ] Regenerate only VO cues whose text content changed, using the existing hash
      pipeline; archive the touched cue list with the review finding.
- [ ] Return one consolidated response to reviewers. One clarification/fix round
      plus one final verification round is the maximum for this preparation
      effort; additional disagreement becomes an explicit open item.
- [ ] Keep issue #23 open until written reviewer sign-off is on file and all
      agreed script/VO changes have landed. This preparation artifact alone does
      not satisfy that gate.
